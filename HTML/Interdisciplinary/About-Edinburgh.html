About Edinburgh

Edinburgh is the capital city of Scotland, which is in southeastern Scotland. Edinburgh serves as the location of the Scottish parliament, the central authority of the Church of Scotland, and the residence of a Catholic Archbishop.

Edinburgh contained an estimate of 459 366 residents as of 2011 and has an area of 264 square kilometres, which makes Edinburgh the second largest city in Scotland. King David I sometime in the early 12th century granted Edinburgh the status of a king’s/royal burgh.

Which was a benefit that furthered trade by enabling Edinburgh to work as both a marketplace and as a centre for organized manufacturing especially of cloth. Edinburgh became a city in 1329 and became the capital city of Scotland after Perth in 1437.

Edinburgh fun facts

1. Edinburgh was the first city in the world to have it’s own fire brigade

2. In Edinburgh there are 112 parks and a higher number of trees per person than any other city in the UK

3. J.K Rowling wrote parts of Harry Potter in an Edinburgh café called “The Elephant”, drawing inspiration from from the surrounding landscape for her characters and settings

4. The Encyclopedia Britannica was originally created in Edinburgh

5. The extinct Volcano Castle Rock where Edinburgh Castle is built upon is 340 million years old

6. The world’s only knighted penguin, Sir Nils Olav, lives in Edinburgh Zoo. The knighthood of Sir Nils Olav was approved by King Harald V and is a part of the Norwegian Kings Guard.
